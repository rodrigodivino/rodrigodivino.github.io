Parallel bundling

The Parallel Bundling is a data visualization technique that uses edge bundling in parallel coordinates to enable categorical analysis

A typical parallel coordinates can view many dimensions at once by laying the axes in parallel, with crossing lines to represent the data points:

However, this visualization is highly cluttered, with overlapping lines hiding the information. This problem is worse in categorical axes, since most of the axis is unused, which increases the density of the lines on the screen.

Parallel Bundling uses two techniques to solve this problema: (1) stretching of categorical axes, and (2) edge bundling

The algorithm of parallel bundling stretches the categorical axes, so that the density of lines significantly reduces at those areas:

The edge bundling enables grouping similar lines. To do that, the user clicks on a categorical axis, which groups the lines in each category of that data dimension. The lines tend to the centroid of the group, showing the features of classes. This is the final result:

The Parallel Bundling is available as a Web App at https://rodrigodivino.github.io/parallel-bundling/ ). The app is static, so your data never leaves your computer.
